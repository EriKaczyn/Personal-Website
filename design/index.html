Design

"Baby Bird Learns to Fly"

In a class called "Constructing the Moving Image", I used Blender to create two characters and an environment for this short comic. I paid special attention to lighting and camera location to express the baby bird's emotions and perspective. I used warmer colors for the bird's familiar, safe environment and cooler colors for the dark, unfamiliar environment. I love how the colors look together. Additionally, I used Unity and C# to create an interactive version of the scene, in which the player takes on the role of the bird and must find the deer and follow her to the nest. This gave me the opportunity to practice making cutscenes and animations for my custom character models.

A-Frame 3D Scene

In my Introduction to Computational Media class, I used HTML and A-Frame to make this 3D scene of a cherry blossom tree. Follow this link to navigate the 3D scene. Since I was new to A-Frame, I went for a simplistic look. The tree sits on a cylinder of grass. Its trunk is made of cones that taper slightly instead of coming to a point. Clusters of flowers are represented by dodecahedrons of various sizes, and individual flower petals in the form of small cylindrical disks float around the tree. I thought these floating petals would be a good way to take advantage of the three-dimensional medium.
